## Key Improvements Made

### 1. **Tightened Output-Focused Language (Lines 110-112)**

**BEFORE:**
"Your stats aren't telling you you're a failure. They're providing objective feedback so you can refine your approach. The numbers serve *you* – they're a feedback loop for your internal operating system."

**AFTER:**
"Your stats aren't telling you you're a failure. They're showing you exactly where to focus your effort next. The numbers serve *you* – they're a feedback loop for getting better."

**Why:** The original had slightly academic language ("objective feedback," "internal operating system"). The edit makes it more direct and teen-friendly while maintaining the same concept.

---

### 2. **Sharpened Jensen Huang Section (Line 112)**

**BEFORE:**
"The moment the numbers become the goal, you've lost sight of what actually matters. You're not tracking numbers to worship numbers. You're tracking to understand reality, so you can change it."

**AFTER:**
"The moment the numbers become the goal, you've lost. You're not tracking numbers to worship numbers. You're tracking to see reality clearly, so you can change it."

**Why:** "You've lost" is more punchy than "you've lost sight of what actually matters." Also changed "understand reality" to "see reality clearly" for more active, visual language.

---

### 3. **Streamlined Repetitive Phrasing (Line 32)**

**BEFORE:**
"You track reps, sets, and progress."

**AFTER:**
"You track reps, sets, progress."

**Why:** Removed unnecessary "and" for tighter rhythm. Minor but improves flow.

---

### 4. **Removed One Word for Better Flow (Line 155)**

**BEFORE:**
"Or you can study for 45 focused minutes, actively testing yourself, and teaching the concepts to someone else."

**AFTER:**
"Or you can study for 45 focused minutes, actively testing yourself, teaching the concepts to someone else."

**Why:** Removed "and" before "teaching" to improve sentence rhythm and avoid slight stumble when read aloud.
